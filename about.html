Our History

BMW HEADQUARTERS

BMW's headquarters, known as the "BMW Four-Cylinder" building, is an iconic structure located in Munich, Germany. Completed in 1972, the building was designed by Austrian architect Karl Schwanzer and resembles four interconnected cylinders, reflecting the design of a car engine. Adjacent to the headquarters is the BMW Museum, showcasing the brand's history and innovations. The building stands as a symbol of BMW's engineering excellence and is a significant architectural landmark in Munich.

THE BMW LEGACY

THE BMW LEGACY is a story of precision engineering, innovative design, and a relentless pursuit of excellence. Since its inception in 1916, BMW has evolved from an aircraft engine manufacturer into a global leader in luxury automobiles and motorcycles. Our legacy is built on a foundation of craftsmanship, cutting-edge technology, and a deep passion for the art of driving. Each BMW vehicle embodies a century of experience, reflecting our commitment to delivering the ultimate driving experience to enthusiasts around the world.
